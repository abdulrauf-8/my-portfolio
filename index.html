// abdirauf hassan

Builder by training, writer by conviction.

Fourth-year Bachelor of Computer Science student at Kenya Methodist University (KEMU), studying Islamic Psychology independently online alongside it. Currently working through a cybersecurity industrial training program at DecodeLabs (Batch 2026).

Comfortable in Python, security auditing, and turning a training brief into a shipped tool — password strength checkers, phishing triage systems, encryption utilities, blue-team audit frameworks. Every project ships with documentation, not just code.

Alongside that, writes long-form essays at the intersection of Islamic spirituality, classical scholarship, and modern psychology — drawing on Ibn al-Qayyim, al-Ghazālī, Izetbegović, and Dostoevsky to think through questions of silence, discipline, and the self. Published on Substack under the pen name Sibiyan at sibiyanwrites.
